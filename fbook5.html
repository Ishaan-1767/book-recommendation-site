The Catcher in the Rye

Author: J.D. Salinger

About the Book

The Catcher in the Ryeis a novel by J.D. Salinger, first published in 1951. A seminal work of modern American literature, the book captures the angst, alienation, and rebellion of adolescence. Through its candid and conversational style, it offers a deeply personal glimpse into the mind of a disenchanted teenager grappling with the complexities of growing up. It has resonated with readers across generations for its honest portrayal of the challenges of identity and belonging.


Plot Summary

The story follows 16-year-old Holden Caulfield, who has been expelled from yet another prestigious boarding school. Narrated from a mental institution where Holden is undergoing treatment, the novel recounts the events that transpired over a few turbulent days in New York City.

Disillusioned with the "phoniness" of the adult world, Holden roams the city, encountering a mix of sympathetic and unsympathetic characters.
