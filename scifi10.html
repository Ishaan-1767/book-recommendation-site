Altered Carbon

Author: Richard K. Morgan

About the Book

Altered Carbon is a cyberpunk science fiction novel by Richard K. Morgan, first published in 2002. It is the first book in the Takeshi Kovacs series and is set in a dystopian future where consciousness can be digitized, stored, and transferred between bodies, effectively rendering humans immortal. Known for its gritty noir atmosphere, philosophical depth, and fast-paced action, the novel explores themes of identity, mortality, and the ethical dilemmas of advanced technology.


Plot Summary

The story follows Takeshi Kovacs, a former elite soldier turned mercenary, who is "re-sleeved" into a new body on Earth after his consciousness is stored for decades. Kovacs is hired by Laurens Bancroft, a wealthy and influential "Meth" (short for Methuselah), who suspects he was murdered despite evidence suggesting suicide.

As Kovacs delves into the mystery, he uncovers a web of corruption, deception, and hidden agendas that spans the upper echelons of society. His investigation takes him through the seedy underworld of Bay City (formerly San Francisco), where he faces violent criminals, shady corporations, and moral dilemmas tied to the nature of immortality.

At its core, the novel is a fusion of hardboiled detective fiction and speculative science fiction, with Kovacs navigating a morally ambiguous world where the rich and powerful exploit technology to escape the consequences of death, while others struggle to survive in disposable bodies.
