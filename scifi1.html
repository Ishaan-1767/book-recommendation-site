Dune

Author: Frank Herbert

About the Book

Dune is a science fiction masterpiece by Frank Herbert, originally published in 1965. The novel is the first in a series and has become one of the most influential works in the genre, winning both the Hugo and Nebula Awards. Set in a distant future, Dune is a sprawling tale of political intrigue, ecological survival, and the rise of a reluctant hero. Known for its complex characters and deep themes, the book explores the intersection of power, religion, and the environment.


Plot Summary

The story takes place on the desert planet of Arrakis, also known as Dune, the only source of the most valuable substance in the universe: spice melange. Spice is essential for space travel, granting heightened awareness and extending life. When the noble House Atreides is granted stewardship of Arrakis, young Paul Atreides and his family move to the planet, only to be caught in a deadly web of betrayal orchestrated by their enemies, House Harkonnen.

After tragedy strikes, Paul and his mother, Lady Jessica, flee into the desert and take refuge with the Fremen, the native people of Arrakis.
