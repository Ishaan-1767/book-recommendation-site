Foundation

Author: Isaac Asimov

About the Book

Foundation is a seminal science fiction novel by Isaac Asimov, first published as a series of short stories in the 1940s and later compiled into a book in 1951. It is the first novel in the Foundation Series, which is widely regarded as one of the most influential works in science fiction. The book combines elements of politics, history, and sociology with speculative science, telling the epic story of the rise and fall of civilizations.


Plot Summary

Set in a distant future where humanity has colonized the galaxy, the novel begins with Hari Seldon, a mathematician who develops psychohistory, a predictive science that uses mathematics to foresee the future behavior of large populations. Seldon predicts the imminent collapse of the Galactic Empire, an event that will plunge the galaxy into 30,000 years of chaos and darkness.

To shorten this period to a mere thousand years, Seldon establishes the Foundation, a group of scientists and scholars tasked with preserving knowledge and laying the groundwork for a new and more stable civilization. Located on the remote planet of Terminus, the Foundation faces challenges from the decaying Empire, rogue warlords, and internal strife.

Each chapter follows pivotal moments in the Foundation's history as it adapts to external threats, using wit, strategy, and Seldon‚Äôs psychohistory to guide its actions. The story explores themes of power, the cyclical nature of history, and the role of individuals versus societal forces.
