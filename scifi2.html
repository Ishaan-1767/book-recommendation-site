About the Book

Neuromancer is a groundbreaking science fiction novel by William Gibson, first published in 1984. It is the inaugural book in Gibson‚Äôs Sprawl Trilogy and is widely regarded as a seminal work in the cyberpunk genre. The novel introduced readers to the concept of "cyberspace" and influenced countless works in science fiction, technology, and pop culture. Known for its gritty atmosphere, intricate plot, and prescient vision of the future, Neuromancer remains a cornerstone of speculative fiction.


Plot Summary

The story follows Henry Case, a washed-up console cowboy (computer hacker) living in the dystopian underworld of Chiba City. Once a talented cyberspace operator, Case is rendered incapable of jacking into cyberspace after his nervous system is damaged by former employers. Penniless and desperate, he is approached by a mysterious figure named Armitage, who offers him a chance to restore his abilities in exchange for completing a high-stakes mission.

Teaming up with Molly, a street samurai with cybernetic enhancements, Case is tasked with hacking into the AI network of a powerful corporation. As the mission unfolds, Case is drawn into a web of intrigue involving artificial intelligence, corporate conspiracies, and questions about the nature of humanity. The climax takes place in cyberspace, where Case must confront not only the entities he‚Äôs been hired to manipulate but also his own fractured psyche.
