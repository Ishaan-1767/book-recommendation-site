The Three-Body Problem

Author: Liu Cixin

About the Book

The Three-Body Problem is a hard science fiction novel by Liu Cixin, originally published in Chinese in 2008 and translated into English by Ken Liu in 2014. It is the first book in the Remembrance of Earth‚Äôs Past trilogy and won the prestigious Hugo Award for Best Novel in 2015. Combining advanced scientific concepts with a gripping narrative, the novel explores humanity's first contact with an alien civilization and the moral and existential dilemmas that arise from it.

The novel delves deeply into astrophysics, game theory, and philosophical questions about survival, cooperation, and trust.


Why Read This Book?

The Three-Body Problem is a thought-provoking exploration of science, morality, and humanity's place in the universe. Liu Cixin's meticulous attention to scientific detail creates a richly immersive and intellectually stimulating narrative. The novel tackles big ideas, including the nature of civilizations, the consequences of first contact, and the fragility of human existence. If you enjoy hard science fiction, intricate storytelling, and global perspectives, this book offers a fresh and compelling voice in the genre.
