To Kill a Mockingbird

Author: Harper Lee

About the Book

To Kill a Mockingbird is a Pulitzer Prize-winning novel by Harper Lee, first published in 1960. Set in the racially segregated American South during the 1930s, the book is a profound exploration of morality, justice, and human nature. Told through the eyes of young Scout Finch, it delves into the deep-seated prejudices of society while celebrating compassion, empathy, and the courage to stand against injustice. The novel remains a cornerstone of American literature, often studied in schools for its timeless themes and rich characters.


Plot Summary

The story takes place in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama, where Scout Finch lives with her older brother, Jem, and their widowed father, Atticus, a lawyer. Scout narrates her childhood experiences, recounting moments of innocence, discovery, and disillusionment.

The story is interwoven with their adventures involving Boo Radley, a reclusive neighbor who ultimately plays a pivotal role in their understanding of human kindness and bravery.
