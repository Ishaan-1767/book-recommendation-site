The Cuckoo's Calling

Author: Robert Galbraith

About the Book

The Cuckoo's Calling is a mystery novel written by Robert Galbraith, a pseudonym for J.K. Rowling, published in 2013. It is the first book in the Cormoran Strike series, introducing readers to the hard-nosed private detective Cormoran Strike and his determined assistant, Robin Ellacott. The novel blends elements of classic detective fiction with contemporary issues, offering a fresh take on the genre with complex characters and a richly woven plot.


Plot Summary

The story follows Cormoran Strike, a struggling private investigator with a complicated personal life and a dwindling business. His fortunes change when a high-profile case lands in his lap: the apparent suicide of model Lula Landry, who fell from her luxury apartment's balcony. Strike's sharp instincts and gritty determination lead him to dark corners of society where nothing is as it seems. The case takes unexpected twists, and the answer to Lula's death reveals more about those around her than anyone could have anticipated.
