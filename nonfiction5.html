The Wright Brothers

Author: David McCullough

About the Book

The Wright Brothers is a captivating biography by David McCullough, published in 2015. It tells the inspiring story of Wilbur and Orville Wright, two brothers from Dayton, Ohio, who revolutionized transportation by inventing and successfully flying the first powered airplane in 1903. McCullough draws from letters, diaries, and other archival materials to craft a vivid narrative that highlights their determination, ingenuity, and resilience in the face of skepticism and adversity.

Wilbur and Orville, raised in a family that valued learning and curiosity, were self-taught engineers who shared an intense passion for mechanics. They first gained success in the bicycle business, which helped fund their aeronautical experiments.

Despite widespread doubt from the public and scientific community, the Wright brothers made history on December 17, 1903, with the first controlled, sustained flight of a powered aircraft.
